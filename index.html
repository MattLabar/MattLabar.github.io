Intro

My name is Matt LaBar and I am an Industrial Design student at James Madison University set to graduate in May 2024. Throughout my academic journey, I have developed a strong passion for design, art, and shoemaking.

I have been fortunate enough to hone my craft through hands-on learning and access to the latest design software and technologies. This has allowed me to bring my creative vision to life and develop intricate and innovative designs. My passion for shoemaking also allows me to understand materials, construction, and ergonomics and integrate them in My Designs.

As I near graduation, I am eager to begin my professional career and contribute my talents to a company or organization that shares my passion for design and innovation. I am excited to take on new challenges and collaborate with like-minded individuals to make a positive impact in the world of design.

I believe that my passion, hard work, and attention to detail make me a valuable asset to any team. And I am ready to take on new opportunities and grow as a professional in my field.

Bow process

The process began with the design of the handle, followed by the bending of wood and shaping to ensure functionality. I then moved on to attaching veneers and bending them into place using a hand-made mold on the CNC. After that, I focused on the final sanding and shaping of the bow, and attaching the string.

Bow Final

The final design of this bow is not just a beautiful object, but a functional piece of equipment that has been put to the test. It has been tested on targets and it performs exceptionally well, proving its effectiveness and efficiency. The result is not only a visually pleasing design but also a high-performance tool.

Kumiko

Kumiko Process

Exploring the traditional Japanese technique of Kumiko was an incredibly rewarding experience. The project was a journey that began with creating custom jigs to achieve the desired angles for the thin wood pieces. From there, I proceeded to construct a frame for the grid section using the traditional mortise and tenon joint method.

As I worked through each step, I was struck by the precision and patience required to master this ancient practice. The final product not only showcased my technical skills but also my appreciation for the history and tradition of Kumiko. I am excited to continue learning and experimenting with this technique in the future.

Camping

As part of my studio class, I was given the challenge of creating a camping product for a specific tool brand, incorporating their brand language and style. Through comprehensive research and feedback from avid campers, I pinpointed the problem of uneven sleeping surfaces while camping. To tackle this issue, I developed a sleeping pad that featured multiple air pockets on the bottom, which could be inflated and deflated to adapt to different terrains. It was an engaging project that highlighted my skills in product design and problem-solving.

About

I grew up in Yardley, Pennsylvania, a suburb of Philadelphia, where I discovered my passion for sports and art at a young age. I played various sports throughout middle and high school, but it was Ultimate Frisbee that ultimately captured my heart. I now play professionally with the New York Empire and was even selected for Team USA in 2020. My love for Frisbee has not only given me lifelong friends and incredible experiences, but it has also opened many doors for me, including the opportunity to travel the world.


In high school, I also became deeply interested in sneaker culture and started a sneaker design business. This interest led me to switch my major from marketing to industrial design at James Madison University. As a designer, I love the challenge of creating new and innovative products, and I am particularly interested in fashion and footwear design. I am excited to showcase my skills and continue learning in the design industry upon graduation in May 2024.
